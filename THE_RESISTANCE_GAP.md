# The Resistance Gap

**Context:** Embodied Trace Decoding (ETD) within SkillOS.

When you train a user via SkillOS, the system perfectly captures the *geometry* of the gesture. Through continuous spatial tracking, it records:
- Angle mapping
- Fulcrum stability
- Instrument velocity
- Rest tremor

*Note: ETD is generalized. Its entry points are baseline fine-motor actions (phone swiping, guitar playing) before graduating to high-stakes clinical tissue.*

## The Biological Friction
In a live environment, particularly the clinical ward, the gesture is resisted by **The Meat**.
A perfect 45-degree mirror angle in the simulator becomes a battleground in the mouth. The clinician must continuously account for pooling saliva, an uncooperative tongue, and sudden patient movement.

## Phase 2: The 360-Degree Assessment
We do not surrender to this friction by calling it "uncomputable." 
The isolated spatial geometry captured in Phase 1 is merely the *Notice* stage of the algorithm. 

**The Directive:** The biological friction is the final frontier of ETD computation. We must execute a 360-degree assessment. Future modules of ETD must quantify "The Meat" through force-feedback integration, moisture resistance modeling, and dynamic pressure tracking. The skill is not just tracing the spatial path, but algorithmically mapping how the human hand maintains the trace *against* measurable biological resistance.
